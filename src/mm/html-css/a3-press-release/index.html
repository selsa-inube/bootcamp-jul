PRESS RELEASE

September 13, 2022

Apple's global phenomenon, “Ted Lasso”, joins the ranks of the most celebrated comedies in history with two consecutive Emmy Awards for Best Comedy Series at the 74th Primetime Emmy Awards

Ted Lasso” once again becomes the most award-winning comedy Emmy
Apple TV+ wins nine total Emmy Awards, including four wins for “Ted Lasso” and honors for “Carpool Karaoke: The Series,” “Severance,” “Schmigadoon!” and “Home Before Dark”

“Ted Lasso” took home four wins at the 74th Primetime Emmy Awards, including back-to-back wins for Outstanding Comedy Series for both its first and second seasons.

CULVER CITY, CALIFORNIA Tonight at the 74th Annual Awards Primetime Emmy, the beloved and successful Apple TV+ series “Ted Lasso” is joined the ranks of comedy legends with his victory as Best comedy series for its first and second seasons, becoming the eighth series of the genre in 74 years of history of the Emmys to achieve it. The second season of “Ted Lasso” is also became the most Emmy-winning comedy for the second year consecutive with four wins in total, including Emmy Awards for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Comedy Series (Jason Sudeikis), Best Supporting Actor in a Comedy Series (Brett Goldstein) and Best directing a comedy series (MJ Delaney).

Apple's “Ted Lasso” now joins the ranks of only seven others comedy series in history that won awards for Best Series of Comedy in its first two years: “Modern Family”, “30 Rock”, “Frasier,” “The Golden Girls,” “Cheers,” “All in the Family,” and “The Phil Silvers Show.”

“We are very grateful and honored to see that 'Ted Lasso' has been awarded two consecutive awards for best comedy, since the Audiences around the world continue to love the show and these characters as much as we do,” said Zack Van Amburg, director of World Video for Manzana. “It has been a great honor for all of us at Apple to collaborate with the exceptionally talented cast and creative team to bring this touching, funny and kind series to the screen. We appreciate sincerely to the Television Academy for recognizing the program and We appreciate everyone for believing in us.”

“It's incredibly gratifying to see 'Ted Lasso' join the ranks of legendary comedies with awards for best comedy in their first two seasons,” said Jamie Erlicht, director of Worldwide Video at Apple. “We send our warmest congratulations to the entire cast and the team, and to Jason, Brett and MJ for their individual Emmy Awards. We thank the Television Academy and also our spectacular teams that have worked tirelessly to bring this very special series to viewers around the world”

In total, Apple TV+ earned nine Emmy Awards this year, including five Creative Arts Emmy Awards, with “Carpool Karaoke: The Series” obtaining the top honor for Best Short Form Comedy, its fifth consecutive victory; the celebrated nominee for Best Series Dramatic “Severance” making its Emmy debut with two wins, including Best Musical Composition for a Series and Best Design of Main Title; the acclaimed “Schmigadoon!” of Apple winning the Best Original Music and Lyrics; and the mystery drama series “Home Before Dark” obtaining the award previously announced by the jury for Best Motion Design.

In addition to this year's honors for the second season of "Ted Lasso", the first season of the series also made history in the 73rd Primetime Emmy Awards last year as the comedy series of first year most nominated in history, obtaining seven awards in total, including Outstanding Comedy Series, Outstanding Lead Actor in a Comedy Series (Jason Sudeikis), Featured Supporting Actor in a Comedy Series (Brett Goldstein), Featured Supporting Actress in a Comedy Series (Hannah Waddingham), Featured Sound Mixing for a Comedy or Drama Series (Half Hour), Image Editing Featured with a Single Camera for a Comedy and Casting Series Featured for a Comedy Series.

Apple's original series, movies and documentaries have already been honored with 275 awards and 1,152 award nominations and counting, including this year's historic win at the Oscars when “CODA” was took home the Best Picture award and made Apple the first streaming service to win the category.
